About this project

The main purpose is to give people the opportunity to learn some things about sailing. We (Odhran and Tiernan) love to sail and we would like others to try it out too.

Most of this project was written in HTML5 using Bootstrap CSS to give some layout to the pages. Each lesson is a seperate HTML page, so that people can restart where they stopped. The answers to the quizzes are checked with Javascript.

Weather

Gust

Here is the complicated version. Gust, in meteorology, a sudden increase in wind speed above the average wind speed. Air turbulence around an obstacle causes gusts; they occur frequently over buildings and irregular ground and are less frequent over water. The term gust also denotes a sudden change in wind speed relative to a flying aircraft.

That is complicated version of a gust here is a more simplified version.

A gust is a bit of wind blowing harder than a normal wind.

This is how you see a gust coming.

A gust makes a black line through the water.

When you see that black line coming you can prepare for the gust or you can turn around.

This is how you can prepare for a gust.

There is a few ways the first way is to let out your mainsheet or you can hike.

Hiking

Hiking is where you lean out from the boat to straighten the boat.

Here is a picture of a person doing it.

While hiking one arm is holding the mainsheet and the other arm is holding the tiller extension. There are straps to put your feet in and they are also used to pull yourself back onto the boat if you capsize.

Tacking and Jiving

You could turn away by tacking or jiving.

Tacking is where you push the tiller away from you.

And jiving is where you pull the tiller towards you.

Here is a knots meaning.

The wind barb indicates the wind direction and wind speed. On the otherhand, the term "eastward" means that the winds are blowing towards the east. Wind speed is given here in the units of "knots" (knt). A "Knot" is a nautical mile per hour.

Here is the simplified way again.

Knots are the way you can measure wind speed.
