About this project

The main purpose is to give people the opportunity to learn some things about sailing. We (Odhran and Tiernan) love to sail and we would like others to try it out too.

Most of this project was written in HTML5 using Bootstrap CSS to give some layout to the pages. Each lesson is a seperate HTML page, so that people can restart where they stopped. The answers to the quizzes are checked with Javascript.

Slips, Jetties and Tides

  • This time I will tell you about the slip and the jetty.
  • I will also tell you about the different types of tides.

Slips

There is usually a slip in your harbour.

The slip is a cold grey concrete ramp slanted towards the water.

A slip is used to launch boats into the water.

Jettys

There would also be a jetty.

Another name for the jetty is the pontoon.

The jetty is a lot of wooden planks spaced out from each other.

Near the end of the jetty there is a few planks slanted towards the water to launch sailing boats.

Jettys come in all sorts of sizes.


Tides

There are four types of tides. (But to make this lesson easy I will only tell you two of these tides.)

The two I will be informing you of are called (off shore & on shore.)

  1. Off shore (which I know is a bit obvious) means the tide is not on the shore it is off the shore.

  2. On shore is obvious as well means the tide is very high on the land and that the tide has come onto your slip.

When someone says that the tide is "on shore" you can launch your boat at the slip
