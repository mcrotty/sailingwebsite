This time I will tell you about the different types of tides.

I will also tell you about the slip and the jetty.

There is also a slip in your harbour.

The slip is a cold grey concrete ramp slanted towards the water.

There would also be a jetty.

Another name for the jetty is the pontoon.

The jetty is a lot of wooden planks spaced out from each other.

Near the end of the jetty there is a few planks slanted towards the water to launch sailing boats.

Here are some pictures of a jetty.

Jettys come in all sorts of sizes and the next one has a bike on it.

There are four types of tides. (But to make this lesson easy I will only tell you two of these tides.)

The two I will be informing you of are called (off shore & on shore.)

Off shore (which I know is a bit obvious) means the tide is not on the shore it is off the shore.

On shore is obvious as well means the tide is very high on the land and that the tide has come onto your slip.

When someone says that the tide is "on shore" you can launch your boat at the slip
