About this project

The main purpose is to give people the opportunity to learn some things about sailing. We (Odhran and Tiernan) love to sail and we would like others to try it out too.

Most of this project was written in HTML5 using Bootstrap CSS to give some layout to the pages. Each lesson is a seperate HTML page, so that people can restart where they stopped. The answers to the quizzes are checked with Javascript.

Parts of the boat

The Rudder

The rudder is a flat board that is submerged in water. It is used to tilt the boat and it is positioned at the back.

The DaggerBoard

The daggerboard also known as the centerboard is in the middle of the boat, it makes sure you don't drift away. To stop the daggerboard from floating away, you should tie it on with a rope.

The Tiller

The tiller is a long stick connected to the rudder. The tiller extension is another long stick connected to the tiller so then you don't have to reach very far to reach the tiller.

The Boom

The boom is a metal bar at the bottom of the sail.

The mainsheet

The mainsheet is a rope attached to the boom. When you pull in the mainsheet, the boom get tugged pulling in the sail.

A Bung

A bung is a small, circular knob that you twist on to the stern. You use it to stop water from getting into the boat and take it out to get water out of the boat. You should always check to make sure your bung is in the boat. Look to the right for a picture of a bung.

Bungs are very small and should be minded carefully.
