Mooring a boat

Mooring a boat means "parking" a boat, or more correctly tying up a boat.

Where to Moor

Mooring a boat is quite simple if you know where the mooring place is. So we are going to start with a picture of a mooring place out in the water and a picture of a mooring on a jetty.

It is important to find an appropriate place to Moor your boat as otherwise, it may float away on you. Or it could get damaged as it floats on some rocks or in the way of other boats. Boats can be very sneaky as they are very quiet when moving.


How to tie a knot to moor a boat

Below is an image of how to do the cleat hitch knot with is the most common one used to tie a boat to a jetty.

How to tie

  1. Pull the rope behind the spar.
  2. Pull the rope over the top, and behind the opposite side.
  3. Do the same for the other side.
  4. Tuck the rope under itself and slip onto the spar.
  5. Important: pull the end of the rope tightly.

If the knot is loose then the knot could become undone.
